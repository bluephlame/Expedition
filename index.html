Welcome to Werrikimbe

This years pathfinder expedition is a little bit different, firstly we are combining the North and the south of the conference in a first time event. Next we are changing the style of event, usually we run a point to point line course. This year is a rogaine.

Rogaining is a sport of long distance cross-country navigation, involving both route planning and navigation between checkpoints

find out more

The Event

While rogaining is usually a competative this event is designed for you and your club to streach thier navigation and hiking skills and have a great time exploring the bush. While this is not a competition we will be tracking scores and posting a leaderboard to encourage you to plan a more optimal route and get as many markers as you can

There are 4 categories that you can enter into, whilst these are more so that we can show standings within similar graded teams, you can push yourself as hard as you need

  • Beginers - you are in your early years of pathfinders and have not yet mastered the skills of map reading, compass and navigation. You will likely travel between 10-15Km over the weekend
  • Intermediate - you understant the basics and are now looking to push yourself. You can move comfortably with your pack. You still need help with your map and compass but can find the ridges, gullys and spurs. You will likely travel between 15 and 25Km over the weekend
  • Experienced - Your team is made up of senior pathfinders who know the ropes. Hills are not a problem and you work together to collect as many points as you can. You should be able to copver 25-25Km over the weekend.
  • Advanced - Weight minimisation, Tight navigation, dicipline and speed. The thickest bush would not slow you down. Can your team collect all the markers in the allocated time?

Teams

each club should present teams with the appropriatly skilled leaders and participants. The minimum group size is 4 and the maximum group size is 16. each group should have between 2 and 4 adult leaders included in that number

Points, markers and activities

The map consists of Controls with various points and dificulties, including some activites that will earn you extra points.

Controls are of either 300, 200, or 100 points. Special activites are worth 600 and special controls are worth 500

The special controls will not be marked on your map and will need to be decoded. Please make sure you have your bibles with you as this will require some searching to calculate the grid point.

Out of bounds

Areas of the maps marked out of bounds or not mapped will result in a disqualification. These are for your safety or private property that we do not have permision to use

Please also note any areas of the map marked for special care or avoidence, these will be displayed on the master map at HQ

Avoid the tracks in which are marked Out of bounds as this may spread an unwanted fungus across the National Park

You must not leave camp without!

Because of Potential cold weather and the risk of any group being benighted unexpectidly, you must not leave camp with out the following.

  • A water prrof layer - Raincoat or poncho
  • A Warmth Layer - A Warm Jacket or coat
  • Water (more then enough for the planned trip)
  • Food (enough for planned trip plus snacks)
  • A Radio (per specifications in Comms)
  • A First Aid Kit (One per group)

It is highly recomended that each group carries a current PLB for use in emergencies only. This should only be used when all efforts to contact HQ have failed and it is Monday evening, Or an immediate medical emergency. HQ will endevour to asses any situation and provide medical evacuation for less sever incidents before calling on external assistance.

Campsite curfews and etiquite

Each Day you are allowed to be seeking controls during the following time periods. If you are late your team is penalized at the following rates.

  • 25 points every 15 min, for the first hour. (Total 100 points)
  • 50 points every 15 min, for the secound hour. (Total 300 points, inlcuding previous hour)
  • 100 points every 15 min, after the second hour.

Camp Clean up

When leaving camp there is no checkout, but if you leave a mess that's -1000 points. But Pathfinders always leave the camp as they found it.

Lost or Displaced

If you feel that it is unsafe to contiue to the closest campsite, stop and camp at an appropriate place. Make contact with HQ to inform them of your decision. Please ensure that safety is concidered above making the curfews.

Map Compass and Navigation

For Safety, every hour you will need to report your location, based either from the know wherabouts on your map, or a GPS reading. This will be broadcast over the UHF.
The Safety Bearing is to head North until you hit a track or road. Then west towards the main road into basecamp.

The Map is a custom built map that includes, vector based countours, recently recorded tracks and roads and reasonably accurate vegitation layers. The terain puts us on top of a plateau, you will notice around the edges of the maps very steep drop offs. Some of which are considered canyons. IT has been done with such detail that reality has been known to be wrong from relative to this map.

Getting There

From the North

Travel towards the New Englend Highway, then Walcha (via Armidale)

Lismore, Northern Tablelands, POW, Grafton, Alstonvile

North Directions

The recomended route is to head towards the New England Highway, whatever seems most feisable. Once you have reached Walcha contiue East on the Oxley Highway, B56. for about 54Km.

After 54Km you will see Kangaroo Flat Road, Turn left on to the road and follow this 20Km until it turns into Moorabank Road.

There will be signposts from this point to guide you, but you contiue approximatly another 15Km until you reach base camp.
